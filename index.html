How to make Oat Milk Latte

Ingredients:

  • Espresso roast coffee (ground finely)
  • Oat milk (or any other non-dairy milk of your choice)
  • Sweetener of your choice (optional, e.g., sugar, agave syrup, maple syrup)
  • Latte art (optional, if you want to add a decorative touch)

Equipment:

  • Espresso machine, manual espresso maker, or Aeropress (for making espresso)
  • Milk frother or whisk (for frothing the milk)
  • Latte cup

Instructions:

  1. Prepare the espresso: Use an espresso machine, manual espresso maker, or Aeropress to make your espresso. If using an espresso machine or manual espresso maker, follow the instructions specific to your machine. If using an Aeropress, refer to the Aeropress Espresso recipe for guidance.
  2. Froth the oat milk: If you have an espresso machine with a steamer, follow the instructions mentioned above to steam and froth the oat milk until it reaches around 150 degrees Fahrenheit. If you don't have an espresso machine, you can froth the oat milk using a milk frother, a French press, or a whisk. Heat the oat milk on the stovetop until it is hot to the touch but not simmering (around 150 degrees Fahrenheit). Then, froth the milk using your preferred method until it becomes frothy with small, even bubbles.
  3. Prepare the latte: Pour the prepared espresso into your latte cup. If you like your latte sweetened, you can add your preferred sweetener to the espresso at this point.
  4. Add the frothed oat milk: Slowly pour the frothed oat milk into the latte cup over the espresso. You can create latte art if desired by using a spoon to manipulate the froth on top.
  5. Enjoy your vegan oat milk latte: Once everything is combined, your latte is ready to be enjoyed! Sit back, relax, and savor the creamy and delicious flavors of your homemade vegan latte.

Optional Latte Flavors:

If you want to add some variety to your latte, you can try different flavored syrups like lavender, vanilla, or even pumpkin spice. Just add the desired syrup to your latte before pouring in the frothed oat milk.

Remember to experiment and adjust the method to suit your taste and equipment. With a little practice, you'll be making barista-worthy vegan oat milk lattes right at home! Enjoy!
